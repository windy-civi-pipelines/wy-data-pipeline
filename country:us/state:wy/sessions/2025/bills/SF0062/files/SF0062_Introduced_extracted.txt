SENATE FILE NO. SF0062
Restrooms in publicly funded schools-2.
Olsen, Salazar and Smith, D and
Representative(s) Banks, Bear, Heiner,
Ottman, Pendergraft, Rodriguez-Williams,
Webber and Winter
A BILL
for
1 AN ACT relating to school districts; requiring public
2 school children to use restrooms that align with their sex
3 at birth; requiring reasonable accommodations; providing
4 exceptions; providing penalties; providing definitions; and
5 providing for an effective date.
6
7 Be It Enacted by the Legislature of the State of Wyoming:
8
9 Section 1. W.S. 21-3-137 is created to read:
10
11 21-3-137. Restrooms in public schools; requirements;
12 definitions; penalties; rulemaking.
13
14 (a) As used in this section:
1 SF0062
2025 STATE OF WYOMING 25LSO-0302
1
2 (i) "Coach" means a person, including but not
3 limited to coaches, assistant coaches or athletic trainers,
4 employed by or contracted with a public school who is
5 involved in the teaching, coaching or training of students
6 participating in a school-sponsored athletic activity;
7
8 (ii) "Multiple occupancy restroom or changing
9 room" means a room in a public school building designed or
10 designated to be used by more than one (1) person at the
11 same time, where persons may be in various stages of
12 undress in the presence of other persons. The term includes
13 but is not limited to a school restroom, locker room,
14 changing room or shower room;
15
16 (iii) "School-sponsored athletic activity" means
17 a sporting event that is supported and affiliated with the
18 school;
19
20 (iv) "Sex" means as defined by W.S.
21 21-25-101(a)(iv).
22
2 SF0062
2025 STATE OF WYOMING 25LSO-0302
1 (b) To ensure privacy and safety, each public school
2 district that serves students in pre-kindergarten through
3 twelfth grade in this state shall require every multiple
4 occupancy restroom or changing room to be designated as
5 follows:
6
7 (i) For the exclusive use of the male sex; or
8
9 (ii) For the exclusive use of the female sex.
10
11 (c) Each public school district shall provide a
12 reasonable accommodation to any person who does not wish to
13 use a multiple occupancy restroom or changing room in
14 accordance with the designation required under subsection
15 (b) of this section. For purposes of this subsection, a
16 reasonable accommodation shall include a single-occupancy
17 restroom or changing room.
18
19 (d) This section shall not apply to persons entering
20 a multiple occupancy restroom or changing room designated
21 for use by the opposite sex when entering in any of the
22 following circumstances:
23
3 SF0062
2025 STATE OF WYOMING 25LSO-0302
1 (i) For custodial, maintenance, repair or
2 inspection purposes;
3
4 (ii) To render emergency medical assistance; or
5
6 (iii) If a suitable meeting room or area is not
7 available, a coach may enter a locker room before, during
8 or after a school-sponsored athletic activity, provided:
9
10 (A) All students present are fully clothed;
11
12 (B) The coach shall be accompanied by not
13 less than one (1) additional adult at all times;
14
15 (C) If the coach is the opposite sex of the
16 students present, the coach shall be accompanied by at
17 least one (1) adult employed by the school district and of
18 the same sex as the students present; and
19
20 (D) The adults present shall not be current
21 high school students.
22
4 SF0062
2025 STATE OF WYOMING 25LSO-0302
1 (e) Each school district board of trustees shall
2 adopt policies to provide for disciplinary action for
3 persons who refuse to comply with this section.
4
5 (f) No school district board of trustees shall adopt
6 a policy contrary to the provisions of this section.
7
8 (g) Upon the department of education's finding of
9 noncompliance with the provisions of subsection (b), (c) or
10 (d) of this section, the noncomplying school district shall
11 lose its accreditation under W.S. 21-2-304(a)(ii) and
12 21-2-304(b)(ii).
13
14 (h) A parent or legal guardian of a student enrolled
15 in and physically attending a public school district shall
16 have a cause of action against the public school for
17 noncompliance with the provisions of subsection (b), (c) or
18 (d) of this section.
19
20 Section 2. W.S. 21-2-304(b)(ii) and 21-3-110(a) by
21 creating a new paragraph (xliv) are amended to read:
22

2 (b) In addition to subsection (a) of this section and
3 any other duties assigned to it by law, the state board
4 shall:
5
6 (ii) Enforce the uniform state educational
7 program standards imposed by W.S. 21-9-101 and 21-9-102,
8 and the uniform student content and performance standards
9 established by rules and regulations adopted under
10 subsection (a) of this section and the requirements of W.S.
11 21-3-137, together with student performance indicators
12 established and measured pursuant to W.S. 21-2-204, by
13 taking appropriate administrative action with the state
14 superintendent, including but not limited to the changing
15 of accreditation status and a loss of accreditation status
16 if a school district is found to be in noncompliance with
17 the requirements of W.S. 21-3-137;
18
19 21-3-110. Duties of boards of trustees.
20
21 (a) The board of trustees in each school district
22 shall:
23
6 SF0062

1 (xliv) Ensure that each school district in the
2 state requires every multiple occupancy restroom or
3 changing room be designated for the exclusive use of the

5 districts that are found to be in noncompliance with this
6 requirement shall lose their accreditation under W.S.
7 21-2-304(a)(ii) and 21-2-304(b)(ii).
8
9 Section 3. This act is effective immediately upon
