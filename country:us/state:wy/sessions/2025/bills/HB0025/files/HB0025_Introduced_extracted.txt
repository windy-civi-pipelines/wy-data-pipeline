Vehicle accident reporting-amendments.
Sponsored by: Joint Transportation, Highways & Military
Affairs Interim Committee
A BILL
for
1 AN ACT relating to motor vehicles; amending when
2 notification and reporting is required for motor vehicle
3 accidents; and providing for an effective date.
4
5 Be It Enacted by the Legislature of the State of Wyoming:
6
7 Section 1. W.S. 31-5-1105 and 31-5-1106(c) are
8 amended to read:
9
10 31-5-1105. Notice required of driver.
11
12 (a) The driver of a vehicle involved in an accident
13 resulting in injury to or death of any person, in property
14 damage to another or others to an apparent extent of at
15 least one thousand dollars ($1,000.00) or in any vehicle,

1 excluding bicycles or any other vehicle moved solely by
2 human power, becoming so disabled as to prevent its normal
3 and safe operation, shall immediately by the quickest means
4 of communication give notice of the accident to:
5
6 (i) The local police department if the accident
7 occurs within a municipality;, otherwise or
8
9 (ii) To the nearest office of the state highway
10 patrol or to the office of the county sheriff.
11
12 (b) Subsection (a) of this section shall only apply
13 if the accident results in:
14
15 (i) Injury to or death of any person; or
16
17 (ii) Property damage to the extent the vehicle,
18 excluding bicycles or any other vehicle moved solely by
19 human power, is so disabled as to prevent its normal and
20 safe operation.
21
22 31-5-1106. Written reports required of police
23 officers; reporting of vehicles struck by bullets.

10 (ii) Total property damage to an apparent the
