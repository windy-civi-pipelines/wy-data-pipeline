Interstate export of Wyoming horseracing.
Sponsored by: Joint Appropriations Committee
A BILL
for
1 AN ACT relating to pari-mutuel wagering; authorizing
2 agreements for purposes of exporting Wyoming thoroughbred
3 horseracing simulcast signals out-of-state; specifying a
4 fee; and providing for an effective date.
5
6 Be It Enacted by the Legislature of the State of Wyoming:
7
8 Section 1. W.S. 11-25-201(b)(iii), (iv) and by
9 creating a new paragraph (v) and by creating a new
10 subsection (n) is amended to read:
11
12 11-25-201. Pari-mutuel permits; fees and reports;
13 disposition of funds; enforcement of provisions.
14
15 (b) Every Wednesday following any pari-mutuel event,
16 the permittee shall:

1
2 (iii) Pay an amount equal to one percent (1%) of
3 the total amount wagered attributable to historic
4 pari-mutuel events, shown by the report to the commission,
5 to be transferred by the commission to the county and the
6 city or town in which the permittee is located, in equal
7 shares, or to the county alone if the permittee is not
8 located within the boundaries of a city or town. The
9 county, city or town receiving an amount under this
10 paragraph may credit that amount to the state fair account
11 upon a majority vote of the county's, city's or town's
12 governing body; and
13
14 (iv) Pay an amount equal to one-quarter percent
15 (1/4%) of the total amount wagered attributable to historic
16 pari-mutuel events, shown by the report to the commission,
17 to be credited to the legislative stabilization reserve
18 account;. and
19
20 (v) Pay an amount equal to six percent (6%) of
21 the net proceeds from covered thoroughbred horserace
22 revenues, attributable to the interstate export of

2025 STATE OF WYOMING 25LSO-0232
1 pari-mutuel simulcast signals, to be credited to the
2 general fund.
3
4 (n) Notwithstanding any other provision of this act,
5 upon request by a permit holder to approve its interstate
6 export of pari-mutuel simulcast signals of covered
7 thoroughbred horseraces, the commission may enter into an
8 agreement with any entity that is responsible for
9 administering the federal horseracing integrity and safety
10 act of 2020, 15 U.S.C. § 3051 et seq., to provide services
11 consistent with the enforcement of the horseracing
12 anti-doping and medication control program and the
13 racetrack safety program. For purposes of an agreement
14 entered into under this subsection and subject to the
15 availability of funds in the pari-mutuel account, there is
16 continuously appropriated to the commission the state's
17 proportionate share for the horseracing anti-doping and
18 medication control program and racetrack safety program
19 calculated under 15 U.S.C. § 3052(f)(1)(C). The commission
20 shall remit funds appropriated in this subsection to the
21 entity responsible for administering the programs in
22 accordance with federal law.
