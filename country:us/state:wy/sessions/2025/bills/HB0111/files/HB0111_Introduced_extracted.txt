Hit and run-responsibility and penalties.
Sponsored by: Representative(s) Smith, S and Senator(s)
Steinmetz
A BILL
for
1 AN ACT relating to motor vehicles; increasing penalties for
2 failing to stop where an accident involves serious bodily
3 injury or death; adding a knowledge requirement for
4 specified failing to stop offenses; providing clarifying
5 amendments; providing definitions; specifying
6 applicability; and providing for an effective date.
7
8 Be It Enacted by the Legislature of the State of Wyoming:
9
10 Section 1. W.S. 31-5-1101 is amended to read:
11
12 31-5-1101. Duty to stop vehicle where accident
13 involves death or personal injuries; penalties.
14
1 HB0111
2025 STATE OF WYOMING 25LSO-0390
1 (a) The driver of any vehicle who is knowingly
2 involved in an accident resulting that results in bodily
3 injury, to serious bodily injury or the death of any person
4 shall immediately stop the vehicle at the scene of the
5 accident or as close thereto as possible, but and shall
6 then forthwith immediately return to and in every event
7 shall remain at the scene of the accident until he has
8 fulfilled the requirements of W.S. 31-5-1103. Every stop
9 shall be made without obstructing traffic more than is
10 necessary.
11
12 (b) Any person failing to stop or to comply with
13 subsection (a) of this section shall, upon conviction:, be
14 imprisoned
15
16 (i) Be guilty of a misdemeanor and subject to
17 imprisonment for not more than one (1) year, fined not more
18 than five thousand dollars ($5,000.00), or both, if the
19 accident resulted in bodily injury as defined in W.S.
20 6-1-104(a)(i);
21
22 (ii) Be guilty of a felony and subject to
23 imprisonment for not more than five (5) years, fined not

1 more than fifty thousand dollars ($50,000.00), or both, if
2 the accident resulted in serious bodily injury as defined
3 in W.S. 6-1-104(a)(x) or death.
4
5 Section 2. This act shall apply to all motor vehicle
6 accidents occurring on and after the effective date of this
7 act.
8
9 Section 3. This act is effective July 1, 2025.
