Section 1:
2025 STATE OF WYOMING 25LSO-0376
Donated blood-mRNA disclosure.
Sponsored by: Representative(s) McCann, Allemand, Angelos,
Heiner, Locke, Ottman, Pendergraft,
Rodriguez-Williams, Smith, S, Styvar and
Wasserburger and Senator(s) Hutchings,
Pearson and Steinmetz
A BILL
for
1 AN ACT relating to public health and safety; requiring
2 blood donors to disclose vaccination status as specified;
3 requiring blood packaging to be marked as specified;
4 allowing a person who receives a blood transfusion to
5 request the use of certain blood; providing definitions;
6 requiring rulemaking; and providing for an effective date.
7
8 Be It Enacted by the Legislature of the State of Wyoming:
9
10 Section 1. W.S. 35-4-1101 is created to read:
11
12 ARTICLE 11
13 BLOOD DONATIONS AND VACCINATIONS
14
1 HB0152
2025 STATE OF WYOMING 25LSO-0376
1 35-4-1101. Disclosure of mRNA vaccinations for blood
2 donations; labeling; right to request certain blood;
3 definitions.
4
5 (a) As used in this chapter:
6
7 (i) "COVID-19" means as defined in W.S.
8 1-1-141(a)(ii);
9
10 (ii) "Messenger ribonucleic acid vaccine" means
11 a type of vaccine that uses called messenger ribonucleic
12 acid to produce an immune response against a disease.
13
14 (b) Any person who collects human blood donations for
15 the purpose of providing blood for human blood transfusion
16 shall require blood donors to disclose whether the blood
17 donor has received a COVID-19 vaccine or a messenger
18 ribonucleic acid vaccine at any time.
19
20 (c) Blood originating from a donor who has received a
21 COVID-19 vaccine or a messenger ribonucleic acid vaccine
22 shall be conspicuously marked on the packaging of the blood
23 and any paperwork related to the blood.
2 HB0152
2025 STATE OF WYOMING 25LSO-0376
1
2 (d) In a nonemergency situation, as determined by the
3 health care provider providing medical services, a person
4 receiving a blood transfusion shall have the right to
5 request blood based on whether or not the blood originated
6 from a person who has received a COVID-19 vaccine or a
7 messenger ribonucleic acid vaccine, if the requested blood
8 is available at the facility.
9
10 Section 2. The department of health shall promulgate
11 all rules necessary to implement this act.
12
13 Section 3. This act is effective July 1, 2025.
14
15 (END)
