A BILL
for
1 AN ACT relating to the Wyoming gaming commission; amending
2 the composition, qualifications, salaries, officers, duties
3 and powers of the Wyoming gaming commission; continuing the
4 liaisons to the commission; making conforming amendments;
5 repealing provisions; providing for appointments and
6 terminating terms of existing appointments; providing an
7 appropriation; and providing for an effective date.
8
9 Be It Enacted by the Legislature of the State of Wyoming:
10
11 Section 1. W.S. 11-25-101, 11-25-104(a) and (b) and
12 11-25-201(d) are amended to read:

2 (a) The Wyoming gaming commission is created. to be
3 composed of nine (9) persons who have resided in the state
4 for four (4) years and are qualified electors of Wyoming.
5 The commission shall consist of five (5) members appointed
6 by the governor, by and with the advice and consent of the
7 senate in accordance with W.S. 28-12-101 through 28-12-103.
8
9 (b) Each member of the commission shall have not less
10 than five (5) years of professional experience. At least
11 (1) member shall be an enrolled member of the Northern
12 Arapaho or Eastern Shoshone Indian tribe.
13
14 (c) Each member of the commission shall receive an
15 annual salary to be paid in equal monthly installments in
16 the same manner that other state salaries are paid.
17
18 (d) Biennially the members shall elect from among
19 their number a chairman and a deputy chairman who shall
20 serve in these offices for a period of two (2) years.

23 the member may be reappointed or a successor shall be

5
6 (f) Any vacancy shall be filled by appointment by the
7 governor in accordance with W.S. 28-12-101.
8
9 (g) Each member, within thirty (30) days after notice
10 of appointment and before commencement of the member's
11 duties, shall take, subscribe and file with the secretary
12 of state the oath of office prescribed by the constitution
13 of this state.
14
15 (h) No member of the commission or family member of a
16 member, as defined in W.S. 9-13-102(a)(v), shall own or
17 have any personal or private interest in any activity
18 regulated by the commission or in any animal participating
19 in a pari-mutuel event.

21 (j) One (1) senator appointed by the president of the

6 the governor and may be removed by the governor.
7 Legislative liaisons shall be paid salary, per diem and
8 mileage as provided in W.S. 28-5-101 when attending
9 meetings of the commission.

2 beginning with the effective date of this act and ending

5 unobligated funds remaining from this appropriation shall
6 revert as provided by law on June 30, 2026. It is the
7 intent of the legislature that this appropriation be

12 Section 5. This act is effective July 1, 2025.
