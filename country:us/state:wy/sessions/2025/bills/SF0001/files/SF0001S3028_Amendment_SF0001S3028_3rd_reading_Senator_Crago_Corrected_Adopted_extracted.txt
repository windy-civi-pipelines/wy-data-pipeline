6 renumber as necessary:
7
8 "[SPECIAL DISTRICT PROPERTY TAX GRANTS]
9
10 Section 351.
11
12 (a) As used in this section, "special district" means senior
13 citizen service districts, county hospitals, county memorial
14 hospitals, special hospital districts, special fire districts,
15 county commission fire protection districts, fire protection
16 districts, rural health care districts, senior health care
17 districts and any other special district the primary purpose of
18 which is to provide health care, emergency health care services or
19 fire protection.
20
21 (b) There is appropriated fifteen million dollars
22 ($15,000,000.00) from the general fund to the department of revenue
23 to be expended only for payments to special districts under this
24 section. This appropriation shall not be transferred or expended
25 for any other purpose. Any unexpended, unobligated funds remaining
26 from this appropriation shall not revert until June 30, 2026. It
27 is the intent of the legislature that this appropriation not be
28 included in the standard budget of the department of revenue for
29 the immediately succeeding fiscal biennium.
30
31 (c) The department of revenue shall establish a grant
32 application and program for distribution of the funds appropriated
33 in subsection (b) of this section. Funds appropriated in subsection
34 (b) of this section shall be distributed for purposes of restoring
35 revenue directly attributable to legislation enacted during the
1
1 2025 general session exempting or otherwise reducing residential
2 property tax assessments for special districts from tax year 2025.
3
4 (d) Only special districts as defined in subsection (a) of
5 this section shall qualify for grants under this section.
6
7 (e) To receive a grant under this section, a county shall
8 prepare a grant application to the department of revenue on behalf
9 of the special districts in the county. If awarded grant funds
10 under this section, the county shall distribute the funds to the
11 special districts in an amount equal to the demonstrated reduction
12 in residential property tax assessments for the special districts
13 directly attributable to legislation enacted during the 2025
14 general session exempting or otherwise reducing residential
15 property tax assessments.
16
17 (f) Grants awarded under this section shall not exceed an
18 amount equal to the demonstrated reduction of residential property
19 tax assessments directly attributable to legislation exempting or
20 otherwise reducing residential property tax assessments enacted
21 during the 2025 general session. If funds appropriated under
22 subsection (b) of this section are insufficient to restore the
23 demonstrated reduced residential property tax assessments under
24 this section, the department of revenue shall make the grant awards
25 on a pro rata basis.
26
27 (g) No special district shall receive more than one (1) grant
28 for any tax year for the purposes specified in this section.
29
30 (h) The department of revenue shall administer the program
31 under this section and shall promulgate all rules necessary to
32 implement this section.
33
34 (j) This section shall only be effective if:
35
36 (i) At least one (1) of 2025 House Bill 0130, 2025 House
37 Bill 0169, 2025 House Bill 0282, 2025 House Bill 0290, 2025 House
38 Bill 0328, 2025 Senate File 0069, 2025 Senate File 0136, 2025
39 Senate File 0153, 2025 Senate File 0161 or 2025 Senate File 0182
40 is enacted into law; and
41
42 (ii) Within any of the bills specified in paragraph (i)
43 of this subsection that are enacted into law, there is no provision
44 providing compensation to local governments for the reduction of
45 residential property taxes resulting from the enactment of the
46 bills.".
47
