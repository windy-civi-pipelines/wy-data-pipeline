Commercial driver's licenses-revisions.
Sponsored by: Joint Transportation, Highways & Military
Affairs Interim Committee
A BILL
for
1 AN ACT relating to motor vehicles; amending grounds for
2 disqualification from driving a commercial motor vehicle
3 for persons under twenty-one (21) years of age; authorizing
4 expunged records to be considered for purposes of
5 disqualification from driving a commercial motor vehicle as
6 specified; adding violations that qualify as serious
7 traffic violations; authorizing the department to waive
8 examinations for a commercial driver's license applicant as
9 specified; providing rulemaking authority; and providing
10 for effective dates.

11 31-7-305. Disqualification and cancellation; right to
12 a hearing.
13
14 (a) Any person is disqualified from driving a
15 commercial motor vehicle for a period of not less than one
16 (1) year if convicted of a first violation arising from
17 separate incidents of:
18
19 (viii) Driving or being in actual physical
20 control of a motor vehicle while the alcohol concentration
21 of the person's blood, breath or other bodily substance is
22 eight one-hundredths of one percent (0.08%) or more; or

1 (ix) Driving or being in actual physical control
2 of a motor vehicle while the alcohol concentration of the
3 person's blood, breath or other bodily substance is two
4 one-hundredths of one percent (0.02%) or more if the person
5 is under twenty-one (21) years of age and the person holds
6 a valid commercial driver's license or commercial learner's
7 permit at the time of the offense; or
8
9 (ix)(x) Violating any local ordinance or state
10 law in another jurisdiction which is substantially similar
11 to any of the violations listed in this subsection.
12
13 (q) Upon the department receiving credible
14 information that the holder of a commercial driver's
15 license or commercial learner's permit is suspected of
16 obtaining the license in a fraudulent manner, the state
17 shall notify the license holder in writing at the address
18 on file that they have thirty (30) days to contact the
19 department and schedule retesting. The notification shall
20 specify what tests must be retaken. If the license holder
21 does not contact the department, does not take the test
22 required or fails any rescheduled test, the license holder
23 shall have his license cancelled. Once cancelled, the

4 permit motor vehicle for a period of one (1) year.
5
6 Section 2. The department of transportation shall
7 promulgate rules as necessary to implement this act.
8
9 Section 3.
10
11 (a) Except as provided in subsection (b) of this
12 section, this act is effective July 1, 2025.
13
14 (b) Sections 2 and 3 of this act are effective
15 immediately upon completion of all acts necessary for a
16 bill to become law as provided by Article 4, Section 8 of
17 the Wyoming Constitution.
